Questions, answered

The things manufacturers actually ask.

Straight answers. No regulatory hand-waving.

Does PPWR apply to us if we only manufacture, not sell, in the EU?+
Yes. Anyone placing packaging on the EU market is in scope, including importers, fillers, and brand-owners. Pure contract manufacturers feel it through their customers' obligations cascading down the chain.
We're already ISO 14001. Isn't that enough?+
No. ISO 14001 is a management framework. PPWR is product-specific, with hard numeric thresholds (recycled %, void space, recyclability grade) measured per SKU and per batch. Different evidence, different cadence.
How does an OEE platform help with a packaging regulation?+
PPWR compliance lives in the same data you already need to run an efficient line: what material went into which package, how much was wasted, what came off rejected. OEEpulse captures it once and routes it to both your production dashboards and your compliance reports.
Can we deploy on our existing PLCs without rip-and-replace?+
Yes. OEEpulse uses standard protocols (OPC-UA, MQTT, Modbus) and ships with edge connectors for the major brands. Average time from kick-off to first dashboard: 14 days.
What's the penalty for getting PPWR wrong?+
Member states set fines, but the structural cost is worse: non-compliant SKUs get blocked from market, recyclability grade C packaging will be banned outright by 2038, and EPR fees rise automatically with bad grades, every unit, every market, forever.
Vocabulary

The PPWR terms everyone pretends to know.

Quick reference. Bookmark this section.

DPP Art. 11
Digital Product Passport. A stable URL (usually behind a QR code) carrying material composition, recycled content, recyclability grade and restricted substance status per SKU. Mandatory from 2027 for most packaging.
DfR grade Art. 6
Design for Recyclability classification, A / B / C. Derived from substrate separability, contamination risk, color/decoration load. Grade C packaging is banned from market by 2038.
Modulated EPR fee Art. 40
Producer Responsibility fee that scales with how recyclable and decarbonised your packaging is. Grade-A SKUs pay less, restricted-substance flags cost extra. Same SKU, 27 different rates.
Annex II Art. 5
The restricted-substance list: PFAS, lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium. Any positive detection breaks conformity for that SKU.
Mass-balance Art. 7
A chain-of-custody accounting method where recycled content is allocated across a production batch rather than physically segregated. Permitted by PPWR but must be verified.
JRC methodology Art. 6
The European Commission Joint Research Centre methodology for assessing recyclability — the official scoring rubric that produces an A/B/C grade. Implementing act expected in 2026.
Void space Art. 24
Empty volume inside e-commerce or grouped packaging. Capped at 50%. Drives PPWR-modulated fees up when exceeded.
Conformity declaration Art. 38/39
A self-declared, signed document affirming a SKU meets PPWR obligations. Manufacturer retains evidence; notifying bodies audit.
PRO Art. 45
Producer Responsibility Organisation — the national scheme that collects EPR fees on behalf of the regulator. CITEO (France), CONAI (Italy), Ecoembes (Spain), ZSVR (Germany).
Substantiation Art. 38
The audit trail that proves a claim. PPWR demands every claim ("30% recycled", "recyclable", "PFAS-free") be backed by verifiable data — not just asserted on the label.
